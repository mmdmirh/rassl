Prospective Students

Thank you for your interest in the Resource Allocation and Stochastic Systems Lab. We regularly post opportunities for motivated students passionate about stochastic systems, queueing theory, and large-scale optimization.

Current Availability

RASSL is not accepting new graduate students or visiting scholars at this time. Please revisit this website in the future for announcements about new openings.

Preparing to Apply

When opportunities reopen, we look for students with strong analytical skills, an aptitude for probability and optimization, and enthusiasm for applying theory to real-world resource allocation problems. We recommend preparing the following:

  • Curriculum vitae highlighting relevant coursework or projects
  • Transcript(s) showing academic preparation in mathematics, statistics, or computer science
  • A brief statement describing your research interests and motivation to join RASSL
  • Any publications, technical reports, or open-source contributions

Stay Informed

Positions are announced directly on this site and on McMaster's official graduate recruitment channels. You may also monitor departmental communications for broader opportunities within Computing and Software.

In the meantime, we encourage you to review our recent publications and ongoing projects to see how RASSL aligns with your interests.

Contact

If you have specific questions that are not answered here, you can reach the lab by email. Please note that we may not respond immediately during periods with no openings.
